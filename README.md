# Superstore Sales Analytics: Retail Performance & Profitability  
**Developed by:** Adarsh Dwivedi | **Role:** Data & Business Analyst  

---

##  Project Overview  
This project focuses on a deep-dive analysis of the **Sample Superstore dataset**, a comprehensive retail dataset covering sales operations across the United States.  
Using **Advanced Excel**, I analyzed over **9,000 transactions** to evaluate regional performance, category profitability, and customer segments.  
The goal is to identify loss-making areas and provide data-driven recommendations to optimize the supply chain and increase overall profit margins.  

---

##  Business Problem  
Elite Retailers (Sample Superstore) faces challenges in maintaining consistent profitability across all product lines and regions. This project addresses:  

- **Profitability Leakage:** Which specific products or regions are incurring losses despite high sales?  
- **Category Dynamics:** How do *Technology*, *Furniture*, and *Office Supplies* compare in terms of ROI?  
- **Shipping & Logistics:** Which shipping modes are most cost-effective while maintaining customer satisfaction?  
- **Discount Impact:** Does high discounting lead to increased volume, or does it primarily erode profit?

##  Methodology & Technical Stack  
- **Tool Used:** Microsoft Excel (Advanced)  
- **Data Cleaning & Engineering:**  
  - Power Query to handle data types & ensure geographic consistency (States/Cities)  
  - Created calculated columns for Profit Margin % and Shipping Duration (Order vs. Ship Date)  
  - Standardized customer segments (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office)  

- **Analytical Techniques:**  
  - Pivot Tables → Aggregate sales & profit by Category, Sub-Category, and Region  
  - Statistical Formulas → SUMIFS, AVERAGEIFS, VLOOKUP for granular data extraction  
  - Trend Analysis → Month-over-month sales growth to identify seasonal peaks

##  Key Insights  
- **Regional Performance:** West Region is most profitable; Central Region struggles due to high shipping costs & discounts  
- **The "Table" Problem:** Furniture → Tables sub-category consistently loss-making despite high sales volume  
- **Technology is King:** Technology products drive highest profit margins; Office Supplies have higher order frequency but lower margins  
- **Shipping Efficiency:** Standard Class is most popular, but First Class shows higher profitability in Technology segment  

---

##  Strategic Recommendations  
- **Operations:** Re-evaluate pricing & discount strategy for Furniture (Tables/Chairs) to prevent profit erosion  
- **Marketing:** Focus promotions on Corporate segment in West & East regions (highest average order value)  
- **Logistics:** Optimize shipping routes in Central region to reduce overhead costs  
- **Management:** Implement a *Bundle Strategy* → sell low-margin office supplies with high-margin technology products
